The Thanksgiving Heat Challenge: Student Guide

Design an Invention to Conquer Holiday Heat Disasters!

Welcome to the **Thanksgiving Heat Challenge!** Your mission is to use your knowledge of heat transfer (Conduction, Convection, and Radiation) to design a theoretical invention that solves a real, messy, frustrating problem that happens during a big holiday meal.

The Initial Hook: Heat Disaster Story

Imagine a heat disaster at a holiday meal: The mashed potatoes you spent an hour making are ice-cold 10 minutes after being set on the table, or worse, a guest burns their hand grabbing a metal dish! Heat is *always* moving, and often at the worst times.

**Your Challenge Start:** Discuss in your group: How is heat moving in your favorite Thanksgiving disaster story? What could stop it?

Your Two-Day Design Process (Pacing Guide)

This is your group's worksheet and pacing guide. Check off each step as you complete it.

Day 1 Focus: Problem and Initial Sketch (45 min)

1.

Review and Hook

15 min

Discuss the heat disaster story and review the 5 key vocabulary terms (Conduction, Convection, Radiation, Insulator, Conductor). Check the "Science Tools" tab if you need a refresher!

2.

Problem Identification

15 min

**Decide on ONE specific problem** your invention will solve. Write your chosen problem here (on your physical worksheet/notebook):

Example Problem: "The cranberry sauce cools too fast because of conduction through the ceramic serving dish."

3.

Brainstorm & Rough Sketch

15 min

Create a rough sketch of your invention. Label its name and basic parts. **Teacher Check-in is required before moving to Day 2.**

Day 2 Focus: Blueprint and Rationale (45 min)

4.

Warm-up & Design Review

5 min

Review your rough sketch and remind yourselves of the two or three science terms you need to use.

5.

Final Blueprint & Scientific Rationale

30 min

Your blueprint must include:

  • Clean, Detailed Drawing
  • **Imaginary Material Callouts** (e.g., "Aero-Gel Insulation")
  • **Heat Flow Arrows** (show where heat travels or is blocked)
  • **Scientific Rationale Paragraph** (use 1 type of heat transfer + 1 Insulator/Conductor term)
6.

Critique & Reflection

10 min

Present your blueprint to another team (2 min each). Write your individual reflection question in your notebook.

How You Will Be Graded (50 Points Total)

Your final score is based on how well you apply the science concepts and present your work. Focus on the Scientific Rationaleit's worth the most points!

Problem & Solution

10 Points

Did you choose a clear heat problem, and is your invention a logical solution?

Detailed Blueprint

10 Points

Is your drawing neat, labeled, and does it use arrows to show heat movement?

Scientific Rationale (CORE!)

20 Points

Did you correctly use at least one type of heat transfer (Conduction, Convection, or Radiation) AND an Insulator/Conductor term to explain your design?

Collaboration & Reflection

10 Points

Did your group work well together, and was your individual reflection thoughtful?
